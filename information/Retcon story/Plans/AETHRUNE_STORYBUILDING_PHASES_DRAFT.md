# Aethrune Storybuilding Phases Draft

This draft extends the current retcon work with storybuilding-focused phases that
should sit after the current implementation-heavy phases in
`AETHRUNE_RETCON_IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md`.

It is based on a scan of the newer lore additions, especially:

## Scan Summary

The newer lore clarifies the campaign's deeper structure.

The setting now has a cleaner escalation ladder:

1. `Emberway`: control of passage
2. `Iron Hollow`: human continuity under strain
3. `Greywake`: control of truth through verification
4. `Hushfen`: truth without structure
5. `Stonehollow Dig`: discovery turning into risk
6. `Resonant Vaults`: systems that learn the player
7. `Abyss`: reality after systems fail to resolve

The new religion file also gives the world a stronger ideological framework:

- Lantern Faith: endurance, memory, connection
- Quiet Choir: surrender to system truth
- Meridian Doctrine: truth through reconstruction
- Ashen Creed: power through control
- Free Path: autonomy against systems
- Remembered: identity through preservation
- Veilbound / Fractured Signal: fringe anomaly and heresy lanes

The newer enemy categories support a cleaner escalation curve:

- early game: human factions and beasts
- mid game: ruin-adapted beings
- late game: system entities and signal-touched threats

## What The New Lore Makes Possible

- a more explicit philosophical arc across Acts 1-3
- stronger companion belief alignment and conflict
- clearer location identities with different kinds of truth pressure
- a better bridge from grounded frontier survival into reality-warping late game material
- more coherent enemy progression tied to region and theme, not species

## Gaps Now Visible

The new files strengthen the setting, but they also reveal missing planning surfaces.

Most important missing pieces:

- no companion belief matrix tied to the new religion/doctrine lanes
- no faction doctrine matrix that maps ideology to methods, scene tone, and quest pressure
- no full parity location bibles yet for `Glasswater Intake`, `Broken Prospect`, `South Adit`, `Blackglass Causeway`, `Meridian Forge`, `Pale Circuit`, and `Meridian Depths`
- no explicit foreshadowing ladder from Act 1 route weirdness to the Abyss
- no quest architecture doc that turns the new themes into actual branch families
- no ending architecture doc for what the final ideological choices should be

## Proposed New Phases

### Phase 9. Truth And Belief Matrix

Goal:
- lock the campaign's philosophical spine before writing more scenes

Core question:
- who gets to define reality when roads, records, systems, and memory disagree?

Outputs:
- a belief matrix for all major factions
- a companion belief alignment sheet
- a location-to-theme map showing what each region proves or challenges
- a tone guide for dialogue written from each doctrine's worldview

Use the new lore to define these axes:
- control vs autonomy
- verification vs lived truth
- memory vs erasure
- repair vs refusal
- order vs unresolved reality

Why this comes next:
- the religions file is now strong enough to drive scene conflict, but it is still not wired into companions, factions, or endings

### Phase 10. Location Bible Completion

Goal:
- bring the rest of Act 2 and Act 3 locations up to the same planning depth as Greywake, Iron Hollow, Hushfen, Stonehollow, and the Resonant Vaults

Priority targets:
- `Glasswater Intake`
- `Broken Prospect`
- `South Adit`
- `Pale Circuit`
- `Blackglass Causeway`
- `Meridian Forge`
- `Meridian Depths`

Each location bible should define:
- overview
- core identity
- first impression
- macro structure
- 8-12 micro-locations
- faction stakes
- gameplay pressures
- symbolic role in the act arc

Why this comes next:
- the current new lore creates strong anchors, but the middle and late-act path still needs parity so the whole campaign feels designed rather than uneven

### Phase 11. Companion And NPC Ideology Pass

Goal:
- make the cast embody the world's belief conflicts instead of only explaining them

Outputs:
- companion doctrine alignments
- key fracture points between party members
- region-specific banter hooks
- quest reaction tables for belief-based choices
- revised major-NPC briefs tied to one clear worldview each

Suggested early pairings to test:
- Elira Dawnmantle -> Lantern Faith
- Bryn Underbough -> Free Path
- Nim Ardentglass -> Meridian Doctrine leaning
- Caldra Voss -> Quiet Choir
- Old Tam Veller -> Remembered
- Varyn Sable / Rukhar Cinderfang -> Ashen Creed pressure lane

Key requirement:
- each companion should disagree with at least one other companion about what "saving" Aethrune means

Why this comes next:
- the current worldbuilding is now richer than the party drama; the cast needs to catch up

### Phase 12. Questline Family Architecture

Goal:
- convert the new world themes into repeatable quest structures

Quest families to define:
- route control quests
- trust and certification quests
- memory recovery quests
- infrastructure repair quests
- signal anomaly quests
- doctrine conflict quests
- unresolved-reality breach quests

For each family, define:
- what starts it
- what pressure it expresses
- what variable it changes
- what moral choice it asks
- what later content it foreshadows

Recommended backbone:
- Act 1 = route control and survival legitimacy
- Act 2 = truth, excavation, and anomaly interpretation
- Act 3 = system choice, resolution failure, and world-order decision

Why this comes next:
- the lore now has strong thematic ingredients, but the quest layer still needs a formal recipe

### Phase 13. System Escalation And Foreshadowing Ladder

Goal:
- make the transition into the strange feel earned

Build a staged escalation model:

1. road residue
- Emberway branch oddities
- fake authority
- path preference

2. structural support without explanation
- Iron Hollow surviving on buried reinforcement
- recurring failure patterns

3. truth instability
- Greywake amendment culture
- maps that shape permission
- controlled records under stress

4. memory instability
- Hushfen contradictions
- names, echoes, and partial warnings

5. buried-system response
- Stonehollow reaction zones
- excavation consequences

6. adaptive architecture
- Resonant Vault pattern learning
- system awareness

7. unresolved reality
- Abyss slips, not full open exploration

Outputs:
- foreshadow placement table by scene and act
- anomaly taxonomy
- rules for when the Abyss can appear and what it is allowed to do

Important guardrail:
- the Abyss should remain a rupture event space, not a normal dungeon replacement

### Phase 14. Enemy Ecology And Boss Identity Pass

Goal:
- align enemy progression, behavior, and bosses with the new location and ideology model

Use the enemy categories doc to lock:
- which threats dominate which regions
- what they say about the local theme
- what boss archetype closes each phase of escalation

Needed outputs:
- region-to-enemy matrix
- boss philosophy sheet
- enemy naming and behavior guide
- mutation/escalation rules for signal-touched and system entities

Desired progression:
- Act 1: human control and frontier predation
- early Act 2: excavation pressure and ruin-adapted beings
- late Act 2: system entities and observation logic
- Act 3: signal-touched and unresolved-reality threats

Why this comes next:
- the world now has a coherent metaphysics; the enemy layer should reinforce it moment to moment

### Phase 15. Meridian Forge, Abyss, And Ending Architecture

Goal:
- define the final choice structure before late-game scene writing begins

Questions this phase must answer:
- what exactly can the Meridian Forge restore, rewrite, suppress, or awaken?
- what does each major doctrine want from the Forge?
- how does the Abyss relate to failed resolution rather than simple destruction?
- what endings exist besides "repair the old system" or "destroy it"?

Minimum outputs:
- final-act conflict statement
- ending families
- faction-end-state table
- companion approval and break conditions
- what the world looks like after each major resolution path

Recommended ending lanes:
- restoration with strict control
- restoration with humane constraint
- distributed autonomy with instability risk
- refusal of total system rule
- containment of unresolved reality at meaningful cost

### Phase 16. Scene Packet Production

Goal:
- turn the planning stack into writing-ready production packets

Each packet should contain:
- scene purpose
- involved doctrines
- active variables
- key NPCs
- required reveals
- optional reveals
- branch points
- companion banter hooks
- enemy mix
- aftermath states

Production order:
- Act 1 revision packets first
- then early Act 2 survey/excavation packets
- then late Act 2 adaptive-system packets
- then Act 3 rupture/endgame packets

## Suggested Immediate Order

If only the next three storybuilding phases happen soon, the best order is:

1. `Phase 9. Truth And Belief Matrix`
2. `Phase 10. Location Bible Completion`
3. `Phase 11. Companion And NPC Ideology Pass`

That order will make later quest, ending, and scene writing much cleaner.

## Practical Recommendation

Do not jump straight from the new lore files into final scene prose.

The scan suggests the world is now ahead of the character layer and the quest layer.
The next pass should therefore wire ideology into people, then bring the remaining
regions to parity, then define the quest families that carry those ideas in play.
